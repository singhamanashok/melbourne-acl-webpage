Overview of ACL Injuries and Rehabilitation

The anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) is one of the major stabilizing ligaments in the knee joint. An ACL injury is a common knee injury that often occurs during sports that involve sudden stops, jumping, or changes in direction. ACL injuries can range from mild sprains to complete tears, and may require surgical reconstruction followed by a comprehensive rehabilitation program.

The ACL rehabilitation process is designed to help individuals regain strength, flexibility, and proper movement patterns after an ACL injury or surgery. It typically involves several phases, starting with initial recovery and progressing through strength and neuromuscular control, running and agility drills, return to sport-specific activities, and injury prevention.
